History of the Game

Scrabble, the renowned word game, was invented by Alfred Mosher Butts in 1938. Initially called "Criss-Crosswords," Butts carefully crafted the game by

calculating letter distribution and point values based on English language frequency. However, it struggled to gain commercial success. In 1948

, entrepreneur James Brunot partnered with Butts to refine and market the game, renaming it "Scrabble." The game gained popularity in the 1950s and

1960s, becoming known for its strategic gameplay and language skills. Scrabble tournaments emerged in the 1970s, and the first National Scrabble

Championship was held in 1971. Today, Scrabble is played in over 120 countries and has numerous special editions, spin-offs, and digital adaptations.
